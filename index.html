What I’m doing right now

I’m prototyping small tools and patterns that make digital life less exhausting — especially for people who deal with ADHD / autism / anxiety, or just a modern calendar that looks like a game of Tetris.

  • Designing for “low-friction capture” Quick entry, predictable navigation, fewer decisions, better defaults.
  • Reducing cognitive load Clear state, fewer options on-screen, “do one thing well” screens.
  • Making tools that feel supportive Gentle reminders, calm visuals, and “stop punishing the user for being human.”

My working philosophy

Neurodiversity-friendly doesn’t mean “simpler for simple people.” It means less hidden complexity, more clarity, and interfaces that don’t demand perfect attention as the entry fee.

If you’ve ever stared at an app and thought “I know what I want, but the UI is making me take a quiz,” you get it.
